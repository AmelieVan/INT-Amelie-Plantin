Christophe Plantin

In the dim glow of his Antwerp workshop, Christophe Plantin sat hunched over a cluttered desk, a quill in hand and ink staining his fingers. The air smelled of fresh paper and metal—his craft’s essence. This wasn’t just a printing house but a hub of Europe’s most turbulent ideas, with Plantin’s story written in the pages he printed, often under another’s name.

By the mid-16th century, the Low Countries were a hotbed of religious and political chaos. Catholics battled rising Protestant movements, while humanists challenged old traditions. To make it as a printer in this storm, Plantin needed more than skill— he had to be shrewd. And shrewd he was.
